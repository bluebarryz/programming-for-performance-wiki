# L32 - Convergence, Ergodicity, Applications

This lecture covers the mathematical foundations needed to apply queueing theory. It begins with convergence of random variables and introduces two approaches to simulation measurement: time averages (Tim's single long run) and ensemble averages (Enzo's many short runs). The concept of ergodicity (irreducibility, positive recurrence, aperiodicity) is introduced to show when these two approaches yield the same result. Little's Law (E[N] = lambda * E[T]) is derived for both open and closed systems. The lecture then introduces Kendall notation and the M/M/1 and M/M/k queue models with their formulas for utilization, completion time, queue length, and waiting time. A detailed performance modelling walkthrough demonstrates how to identify bottleneck devices, calculate maximum transaction rates, and compute average transaction times using visitation ratios and device utilization.
